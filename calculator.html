Understanding W-2 Tax Returns

What is a W-2?

A W-2 form, officially titled "Wage and Tax Statement," is a document you receive from each employer you worked for during the previous tax year (typically by January 31st). It summarizes your earnings and the taxes withheld from your paychecks throughout the year.

Why is a W-2 Important for Taxes?

The information on your W-2 is crucial for filing your federal and state income tax returns accurately. You'll report the amounts from specific boxes on the W-2 on the corresponding lines of your tax forms.
